INSTAGRAM



Instagram began development in San Francisco as Burbn, a mobile check-in app created by Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger. On March 5, 2010, Systrom closed a $500,000 (equivalent to $658,300 in 2022) seed funding round with Baseline Ventures and Andreessen Horowitz while working on Burbn.[13][14] Realizing that it was too similar to Foursquare, they refocused their app on photo-sharing,

Hashtags



In January 2011, Instagram introduced hashtags to help users discover both photos and each other.[154][155] Instagram encourages users to make tags both specific and relevant, rather than tagging generic words like "photo", to make photographs stand out and to attract like-minded Instagram users.[156]
